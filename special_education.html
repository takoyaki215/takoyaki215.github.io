Introduction

The users are the teachers and the students of the schools in America.
The customers still use pen and paper to communicate with their students now. Such communication is like making to-do lists for these students and supervising them to finish it, and that is where my inspiration comes from.

I was responsible for the user experience strategy- "gamification", UI/UX and visual design of the iOS app. Sketching and storyboarding, I generated stacks of ideas about the arrangement of interactive behaviors. I lead the UI/UX work, producing the major deliverables and presenting these to the client. Meeting with teachers helped me to understand the educational challenges. I categorized and the segmented user’s needs into both situations and aligned these content and features.

Conception

The situation is that both teachers and students have an iPad.
Because students are physically and mentally challenged children, the actions have to divide into several detailed steps to finish. For example, hand washing needs to be divided into several steps from applying soap for drying hands.
I used tropical elements and animals to attract children’s interest and make special education no longer dreary and establish a unique style. Moreover, the gamification design encourages children to complete the tasks.

For students

Compare with the teacher, the font and line spacing of student’s mode is cleaner, larger and more space. I think children can be guided step by step to complete basic movements through to disassemble of each movement. Gamification makes them more motivation and fun to use this app.

As Yu-kai Chou indicated the gamification is the craft of deriving all the fun and addicting elements found in games and applying them to real-world or productive activities. This is what I call “Human-Focused Design” as opposed to the “Function-Focused Design.” It is a design process that optimizes for the human in the system, as opposed to pure efficiency of the system. Human-Focused Design remembers that people in the system have feelings, insecurities, and reasons why they want or do not want to do things, and therefore optimizes for their feelings, motivations, and engagement.
